An AI team that operates entirely on its own AI platform — with agents handling ops, research, delivery tracking, and knowledge — is the most credible proof of AI maturity in any organization. We don't just build AI for BU. We run on AI ourselves. That's what makes us the pioneer and the benchmark.

Sprint-based delivery with two mandatory governance gates. The feedback loop is a first-class phase. Agents assist at every phase.

06 · Culture
Values & Culture Code
How we work, not just what we build. Given high management trust and an AI-naive org, culture is as strategic as architecture.
🧪
Experiment Fast, Fail Safe
Bias toward action. Build POCs in days. Fail in dev, never in prod. Postmortems are blameless learning events. Speed and safety are not opposites.
🫂
BU Empathy First
The BU doesn't know what they don't know. Meet them where they are. Translate pain into AI opportunity. No jargon in BU-facing communications.
🏛️
Responsible by Design
RAI, privacy, and fairness are built in — not bolted on. Every solution goes through a governance lens. We earn trust through discipline, not just speed.
🔁
Feedback Loops over Big Bangs
Every deployed solution has a feedback channel. KPIs reviewed monthly. Improve based on data. Shipping a v1 that learns beats waiting for a perfect v1.
📢
Share Everything
Wins, failures, learnings — all shared in the community. Docs are first-class artifacts. Every squad member teaches once a quarter. Agents publish knowledge automatically.
🌱
Lift Everyone Up
Use management trust to pull everyone up. BU champion coaching, workshops, peer mentoring are squad KPIs. The org's AI maturity is our metric too.
⚡
We Run on What We Build
Our agents handle our own ops. Our assistants accelerate our own work. If we don't trust our platform enough to run on it ourselves, why would anyone else? Internal deployment is the most credible endorsement.
🔬
Research as a Habit
We read papers. We run benchmarks. We track what the field is doing. Research is not a separate track — it feeds directly into our build decisions. The Research Agent makes sure we never fall behind.
🏆
Pioneer Mindset
We aim to be the prime example of AI engineering in Thai banking. Not the fastest followers — the ones who others reference. Our architecture decisions, governance frameworks, and agent patterns should be worth sharing externally.

08 · Governance
AI CoE & EA Committee
Governance is not a checkpoint — it's a continuous practice. Two mandatory delivery gates, a structured committee, and a clear escalation path for every severity level.
AI CoE Scope
Owns AI strategy, platform (Agent Fabric), policy framework, community, and center of expertise. Acts as internal AI consulting arm for all BUs. Reports to CTO / Digital Head.
EA Committee (AI)
Reviews all AI use-case proposals before build. Members: AI Lead (chair), Architect, Gov Officer, Risk/Compliance rep, BU sponsor. Meets bi-weekly or on-demand for intake.
AI Policy Domains
RAI (Privacy, Safety, Security, Accuracy, Fairness, Transparency) · Data usage policy · Model approval & auditing · Vendor LLM policy (data residency, SLA, exit) · User-facing AI disclosure (BOT)
Governance Cadence
Monthly: KPI review + RAI audit. Quarterly: roadmap review + policy updates. Semi-annual: maturity assessment. Annual: full external audit + benchmark + published case study.
Internal Platform Support
Orchestrator + subagents handle tier-1 support autonomously. AI Ops Engineer is accountable for agent behavior and SLA compliance. Tier-2+ escalates to AI Ops + Tech Lead. SLA defined per tool criticality.
Risk Escalation Path
AI Engineer → Tech Lead → Architect → EA Committee → Risk Committee (for high-risk). Incident Agent auto-notifies, logs, and starts RCA. All incidents shared in community as learning artifacts.
Model Approval Process
New model / LLM provider requires: security review, data residency assessment, RAI benchmark, Eval Eng sign-off, Architect ADR. No model goes to prod without this trail.
Agent Ecosystem Governance
All internal agents undergo RAI assessment and eval coverage owned by Eval Eng. Permission tiers: read-only = autonomous · write = confirm step · destructive = human-in-the-loop. All agent I/O passes through guardrail service. All traces in Langfuse.
